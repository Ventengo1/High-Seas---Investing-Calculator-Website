What is Investing?

Investing is the process of allocating money to assets with the expectation of generating a return. It's like planting a seed and watching it grow over time.

Savings Account vs. Investment Account Comparison

A Savings Account is a type of deposit account at a bank that typically offers a low but stable interest rate. Your money grows slowly, primarily from the fixed interest earned, and there's little risk involved.

On the other hand, an Investment Account (like one invested in stocks, bonds, or mutual funds) has the potential to earn much higher returns. However, it also comes with a risk of losing value in the short term, as investment returns are not guaranteed and depend on market performance.

Example

If you start with an initial amount of $1,000 and contribute $100 monthly over 10 years:

  • Savings Account: At an average 1% annual interest rate, your account could grow to around $13,000, thanks to compounded interest.
  • Investment Account: At an average 7% annual return rate, your account could potentially grow to over $18,000. However, remember that investments can fluctuate and may not provide a steady rate of return.
